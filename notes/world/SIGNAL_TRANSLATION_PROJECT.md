# The Signal Translation Project

## Overview

An international research initiative headquartered at UC Berkeley, dedicated to decoding the alien transmissions discovered in the cosmic background radiation. The largest coordinated scientific effort since the Human Genome Project.

---

## Origin

**The Chen Discovery (2032)**

A graduate student named Maya Chen, working on signal processing algorithms, made a computational error that revealed structure in what had been dismissed as cosmic noise. The "background radiation" of the universe was not random—it contained layered, patterned signals.

The discovery took months to verify. Years to accept.

When the scientific community finally acknowledged what Chen had found, everything changed.

---

## The Premise

The project operates on a foundational assumption:

> The signals represent communication from extraterrestrial intelligence(s), and with sufficient analysis, they can be decoded and understood.

Implicit in this assumption is a second, rarely stated belief:

> The signals are *related*—different frequencies or channels of a coherent whole, or at minimum, decodable through shared principles.

This assumption has never been proven. It has become too important to question.

## The Work

Hundreds of distinct signal patterns have been isolated from the cosmic background. They vary in:
- Frequency
- Duration
- Complexity
- Apparent age (some appear billions of years old; others are ongoing)

Each pattern is assigned a designation (e.g., "Signal 7," "Pattern 441," "CB-2819") and worked on by teams ranging from 2 to 20 researchers.

Progress has been slow. A few patterns show internal consistency—repeated elements, structural regularities—but no pattern has been definitively "decoded" until Tomasz's breakthrough.

---

## The Politics

The project exists at the intersection of:
- **Science:** The genuine work of analysis and discovery
- **Government:** National security concerns, international competition, public accountability
- **Media:** The hunger for narrative, "first contact" stories, human-interest angles
- **Public hope:** The desperate, unspoken wish that we are not alone

All of these demand a "they." All of these require the signals to *mean something* that can be communicated, summarized, reported.

The project cannot survive the conclusion that there is no unified "they."

---

## The Unspoken Pressure

Everyone working on the project understands, at some level, that their continued funding depends on progress toward a coherent translation.

No one says this directly. It manifests as:
- Preference for research that shows "convergence" between patterns
- Skepticism toward findings that suggest fundamental incompatibility
- Career incentives that reward unification over fragmentation
- A subtle, pervasive optimism that has become difficult to distinguish from dogma

---

## What Tomasz Knows

The first successful decode—his decode—does not generalize.

The elegant solution that unlocked Pattern 7 produces fluent, coherent, *wrong* output when applied to Pattern 12.

The patterns are not dialects of a shared language. They are not channels of a unified broadcast. They are not pieces of a puzzle.

They are unrelated. Local. Contextual.

There is no "they."

And the entire project is built on the assumption that there is.
